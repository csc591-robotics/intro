Results-based conclusions from the current experiment data

1. Flow 5 was the most reliable method in the current dataset.
- Flow 5 reached the goal in both recorded experiments (2/2 success).
- Experiment 1: goal reached in 205.766 s with final distance 0.082 m.
- Experiment 2: goal reached in 247.349 s with final distance 0.052 m.
- This makes Flow 5 the strongest method in terms of consistency.

2. Flow 6 had the best best-case runtime, but it was not reliable.
- The best successful Flow 6 run reached the goal in 80.102 s with final distance 0.239 m.
- However, Flow 6 only succeeded in 1 of 7 recorded runs.
- Other Flow 6 runs either crashed, were rejected by Nav2, or exhausted the step limit.
- This means Flow 6 showed strong potential as a control/Nav2-based method, but its implementation was unstable in the current experiments.

3. Flows 2 and 3 cannot be judged fairly on navigation quality from these results alone.
- Both Flow 2 runs and both Flow 3 runs terminated with Anthropic API rate-limit errors (429).
- Flow 2 success rate: 0/2.
- Flow 3 success rate: 0/2.
- Because these runs failed due to system/provider limits rather than clear navigation mistakes, they should be reported as inconclusive instead of simply worse planners.

4. Among the failed LLM-agent runs, Flow 3 ended closer to the goal than Flow 2.
- Experiment 1:
  Flow 2 final distance = 5.814 m
  Flow 3 final distance = 5.074 m
- Experiment 2:
  Flow 2 final distance = 5.557 m
  Flow 3 final distance = 3.928 m
- This suggests the LiDAR-enhanced setup in Flow 3 may have improved local progress before termination, although the dataset is too small to treat this as definitive.

5. The successful Flow 5 and Flow 6 runs traveled similar total path lengths.
- Flow 5:
  Experiment 1 path length = 14.806 m
  Experiment 2 path length = 14.915 m
- Best successful Flow 6 run:
  Experiment 1 path length = 14.743 m
- This indicates that Flow 6's faster successful runtime did not come from taking a dramatically shorter path; it likely came from a different control/planning behavior.

6. Runtime alone is not enough to rank the methods.
- Some short runtimes came from crashes or early failures, especially in Flow 6.
- Some very long runtimes in Flows 2 and 3 were caused by rate-limit failures rather than long but successful navigation.
- Because of this, runtime should be interpreted together with outcome, final distance to goal, and total distance traveled.

7. There is currently no experimental evidence for Flow 1.
- No Flow 1 experiment metadata was found in the current experiment_data_folder.
- Flow 1 should not be included in any quantitative comparison unless new runs are added.

Recommended wording for the Results section

"Across the recorded experiments, Flow 5 was the most reliable navigation method, reaching the goal in both Experiment 1 and Experiment 2 with final goal errors below 0.1 m. Flow 6 achieved the fastest successful run at 80.102 s, but it was unstable overall, succeeding in only 1 of 7 recorded runs. Flows 2 and 3 both failed because of API rate-limit errors, so their results are better interpreted as system-level failures rather than purely navigation failures. Among these failed LLM-agent runs, Flow 3 consistently ended closer to the goal than Flow 2, suggesting that adding LiDAR may have improved local progress before termination."
